IWD 2019


In collaboration with Women Techmakers Kolkata and GDG Kolkata we had all gathered today to celebrate Women’s Day 2019. Women’s day as is celebrated on the 8th of March isn’t just bound by a day. It’s a celebration of unity, advocacy and action for every day of the year. Today was a day of tribute to the creators of the world and a small token of gratitude to the wonderful women around us. It was a privilege for me to host the event today as I had the opportunity of verbally thanking all the wonderful women whose contributions to the various fields in today’s day is tremendously powerful.

The entire event was wonderfully knit together by Ms. Manisha Biswas who led us into the whole world of women techmakers. What inspired me the most in her speech today was the part where she mentioned “we rise by lifting others”. Today’s world needs women to be so strong that they can be gentle, so educated that they can be humble, so fierce they can be compassionate and so disciplined that they can be free. Its about being those women and inspiring others to do so too. We next had Ms. Prateeti Mukherjee talking to us about “Illuminastick”- a smart healthcare device for the disabled. This wasn’t just about the technical feat that she had achieved, it was also about the thought behind it and that’s what has truly inspired me. It takes a very small idea and courageous mind to execute great work and she stands as an example for that statement. We’ve also had an online session by Jap Leen Kaur Jolly and Baani Leen Kaur Jolly about reinforcement learning, a very in-demand field of tech. This was followed by a beautiful and empowering activity of “I am remarkable” and a very informative speech by Rimjhim Bhadani. Last but definitely not the least we had Rupal Jain as the speaker for Girlscript- Code for Freedom.

These women that I love and admire for their strength and grace did not get that way because things worked out. They got that way because they handled it. In a thousand different ways, on a thousand different days.
